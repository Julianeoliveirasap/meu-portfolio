The Challenge

Structural issues impacting pipeline growth

SAP LATAM manages more than 166,000 corporate accounts across multiple countries and systems. Despite significant efforts in data cleansing and account creation, structural challenges continue to affect sales and marketing — causing teams to operate with incomplete, outdated, or inconsistent information that directly impacts segmentation, campaign effectiveness, and pipeline generation.
  • Inconsistent company classifications, revenue figures, and account ownership
  • Dependence on external providers with incomplete market coverage
  • Missing unique identifiers causing duplicate and inaccurate records
  • Manual and slow account creation processes
  • No unified criteria across regions — decisions vary by analyst
Strategic Vision

Move from a reactive Data Management model to an industrialized Account Intelligence Factory — a scalable data production line that transforms raw market information into sales-ready intelligence across Latin America.

Looking Ahead

Future Innovation Opportunities

Beyond the 6-stage production line, the Account Intelligence Factory can evolve into a more connected, traceable, and automated intelligence ecosystem. These are not additional production stages, but strategic capabilities that can increase scalability, trust, and business impact over time.

01
Account Knowledge Graph

Build a relationship-based intelligence layer connecting companies, subsidiaries, corporate groups, executives, industries, technologies, campaigns, opportunities, territories, and chambers of commerce.

Instead of treating each account as an isolated CRM record, the Knowledge Graph would allow SAP LATAM to understand business ecosystems, ownership structures, market relationships, and hidden connections between accounts.

Expected value

Deeper market intelligence, better segmentation, stronger account prioritization, and improved visibility into corporate ecosystems.

02
Account Data Passport

Create a traceability layer for each account, showing where each data attribute came from, when it was last updated, which source validated it, and how reliable it is.

The Data Passport would document the origin and confidence level of key attributes such as Tax ID, website, legal status, revenue, employees, industry, address, executive information, and enrichment sources.

Expected value

Higher trust in CRM data, stronger governance, better auditability, and clearer decision-making for sales and marketing teams.

03
Agentic Data Operations

Introduce specialized AI agents to support operational orchestration across the data ecosystem. Agents could monitor sources, identify anomalies, validate changes, compare records, check governance rules, suggest next actions, and route exceptions for human review.

This would not replace the data strategy, but act as an automation layer that increases speed, consistency, and traceability.

Expected value

Higher automation, faster processing, lower manual workload, scalable data operations, and more consistent CRM updates.
